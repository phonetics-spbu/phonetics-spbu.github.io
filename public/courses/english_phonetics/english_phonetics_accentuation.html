The collection of exercises supplies material for practising sentence-stress, rhythm and weak forms in English. It is meant for advanced learners with a school knowledge of English and the correct pronunciation of words in common use.

If the speech of the foreign learner is to be a perfect reproduction of that of the native speaker, they must use the special assimilated and elided word forms used in context, observe the rules concerning weak forms, should cultivate the correct variations of word rhythmic patterns according to the context and should make proper use of liaison forms. In any case, whether or not they use such forms themselves, they must know of their existence, for otherwise they will find it difficult to understand much of colloquial English.
